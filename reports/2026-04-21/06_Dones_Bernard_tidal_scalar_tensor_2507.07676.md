## Summary

Using the post-Newtonian multipolar-post-Minkowskian formalism adapted to scalar-tensor theories, Dones and Bernard compute tidal corrections to the total energy flux (gravitational + scalar) and waveform phasing at NNLO (2PN relative to leading dipolar tidal contribution) in the adiabatic approximation. At this order, three independent types of tidal deformability (scalar, tensorial, and mixed) contribute, enriching the observable signature compared to GR. Full waveform amplitude modes including memory contributions are derived to relative 1.5PN order.

---

## Strengths

- **Extends state-of-the-art**: NNLO tidal contributions in scalar-tensor theories — highest order computed for these effects.
- **Three independent tidal deformabilities**: The appearance of mixed scalar-tensorial tidal couplings at this order is a genuine result with observational implications.
- **Complete treatment**: Both flux and waveform (including scalar radiation and memory modes) computed consistently.
- **Solid PN methodology**: Standard multipolar-post-Minkowskian formalism rigorously applied.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Specialized scope**: Scalar-tensor tidal effects are relevant only if scalar-tensor gravity is the correct theory — pure GR is currently consistent with all data.
- **Adiabatic approximation**: Tidal effects computed in the adiabatic limit; dynamical tidal effects and resonances are not included.
- **No numerical validation**: No comparison with numerical scalar-tensor simulations.
- **Companion paper (2310.19679)**: This is the tidal extension of Bernard et al.'s previous point-particle work; the methodology is not new.

---

## Critical Cross-Examination

**Is this genuinely new or incremental?** The extension to NNLO is new and reveals the three-deformability structure. However, the methodology follows the established PN framework — the calculation is technically demanding but conceptually straightforward.

---

## Key Conclusions

1. Three independent tidal deformabilities (scalar, tensorial, mixed) contribute at 2PN in scalar-tensor theories.
2. Memory modes computed for the first time in this context.
3. Results essential for interpreting future high-precision NS binary observations in modified gravity.
