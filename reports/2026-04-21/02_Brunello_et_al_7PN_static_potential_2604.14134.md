## Summary

This paper presents a closed-form formula for computing static post-Newtonian corrections to the two-body gravitational potential at any odd order, derived within a correlation function framework that exploits the Z₂ symmetry of the static sector. As a demonstration, the authors compute the 7PN static potential at O(G_N⁸ v⁰), involving seven-loop Feynman diagrams, finding complete agreement with diagrammatic results. The work provides both structural insight into why static PN corrections factorize and the highest-order static potential ever computed.

---

## Strengths

- **Novel all-order formula**: The closed-form expression for static PN corrections at any odd order is a genuine theoretical advance, providing structural understanding beyond mere computation.
- **Rigorous cross-validation**: Independent agreement with diagrammatic calculations at 7PN validates both approaches — a powerful consistency check.
- **Technical depth**: Seven-loop integration in gravitational EFT is state-of-the-art. The Z₂ symmetry interpretation provides a new angle on the factorization theorem.
- **Steinhoff involvement**: Connects PN-EFT expertise with EMRI/spinning-particle community.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Static limit only**: The 7PN result is restricted to v⁰ terms. Real-world binary dynamics are dominated by velocity-dependent and spin-orbit terms at high PN orders. The practical impact on waveforms is marginal.
- **Incremental progression**: This extends the series 5PN → 6PN → 7PN methodically. The all-order formula is elegant but builds on the existing factorization insight of Foffa et al. (2019).
- **No phenomenological impact assessment**: No discussion of how the 7PN static term affects waveform accuracy, convergence, or parameter estimation.
- **Narrow scope**: Non-spinning, static limit. No connection to spin effects or PN-to-Kerr matching yet.

---

## Critical Cross-Examination

**What is genuinely new?** The all-order formula is the key contribution — it provides a recursive structure that goes beyond the specific 7PN result. However, the formula works only for static (v⁰) terms at odd PN orders, which limits its generality. A skeptic might say: "The formula is elegant but solves only the easiest part of the PN problem."

**Comparison to 6PN (Dec 2025):** The December paper computed 6PN via diagrammatic methods. This paper uses the correlation-function approach for 7PN. The two are complementary, not competitive — but neither addresses the physically dominant velocity-dependent terms.

## Key Conclusions

1. Closed-form formula for static PN corrections at any odd order — structural insight into gravitational factorization.
2. 7PN static potential computed and validated (seven-loop, O(G⁸)).
3. Z₂ symmetry interpretation provides new understanding of the factorization theorem.
4. Practical impact limited by restriction to static, non-spinning sector.
