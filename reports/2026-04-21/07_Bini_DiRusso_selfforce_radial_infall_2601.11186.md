## Summary

Bini and Di Russo compute the radiated energy from a radially infalling particle released from rest in Schwarzschild spacetime at first self-force accuracy, for both scalar and gravitational perturbations. The results are accompanied by post-Newtonian consistency checks. The paper outlines building blocks for future higher-order PN computations and extensions beyond the radial infall case.

---

## Strengths

- **Technically solid**: Bini is a major figure in analytic self-force calculations; the computation follows well-established methodology.
- **Self-force at first order**: Genuine self-force calculation (not test-particle), providing benchmarks for numerical self-force codes.
- **PN cross-checks**: Consistency with post-Newtonian expansions provides independent validation.
- **Building blocks**: The paper is positioned as groundwork for future, more general calculations.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Narrow scope**: Radial infall is the simplest possible orbit — no angular momentum, no eccentricity. The astrophysical relevance is limited.
- **Expected results**: Analytic self-force for radial infall is a well-trodden path. The novelty is incremental.
- **Limited immediate impact**: The "building blocks" framing suggests the paper's main value is in future applications, not the present results.
- **No comparison with numerical self-force codes**: Missing benchmarking opportunity.

## Critical Cross-Examination

**What is genuinely new?** The specific first-order self-force radiated energy for radial infall in Schwarzschild at this level of analytic detail. While technically sound, this extends known methods to a specific (simple) case rather than introducing new physics or methodology.
