## Summary

This paper extends the QNM bilinear product formalism to hyperboloidal foliations, addressing the long-standing divergence problem in QNM inner products. The key finding is that despite QNM solutions being smooth and finite on future-directed hyperboloids, the bilinear product integrand is still divergent due to the CPT reflection operator required in the definition. The authors present multiple regularisation procedures (semi-analytic, complex contour, and flux-inclusive), compute excitation coefficients for scalar Schwarzschild perturbations, and clarify the geometric structure underlying QNM orthogonality.

---

## Strengths

- **Honest structural insight**: The paper clearly establishes that divergences in QNM bilinear products are intrinsic to the formalism (not merely geometric artifacts), resolving confusion in the literature about whether hyperboloidal coordinates alone fix the problem.
- **Elegant geometric interpretation**: Future/past hyperboloidal foliations as dual coordinate systems implementing CPT reflection symmetry — conceptually clarifying.
- **Multiple complementary regularization approaches**: Three strategies are presented with demonstrated consistency, providing practical tools.
- **Strong author team**: Panosso Macedo (leader in hyperboloidal methods), Sberna (known for QNM rigor), Pantelidou (Piovano co-author) — this group carries credibility.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Hyperboloidal approach clarifies but doesn't resolve**: The paper essentially concludes that regularization is still needed — the hyperboloidal framework provides geometric clarity, not a cure. This partially undermines the original motivation.
- **Regularization procedures somewhat ad hoc**: The semi-analytic method exploits Schwarzschild-specific properties. The complex contour method is a standard trick. Neither feels like a natural consequence of the formalism.
- **Flux-inclusive extension incomplete**: Authors acknowledge it "lacks full control over asymptotic behaviour" and falls short of competing methods.
- **Limited scope**: Scalar Schwarzschild only — no Kerr, no tensor perturbations.

---

## Critical Cross-Examination

**Is this new mathematical structure or repackaging?** Primarily repackaging with geometric clarification. The core orthogonality product definition is not fundamentally changed. The novelty is in the geometric interpretation and the demonstration that divergences are structural.

**Comparison to competing methods:** The flux-based extension underperforms compared to alternative hyperboloidal QNM projection methods (refs 38-40 in the paper). This is honestly acknowledged but limits the practical impact.

---

## Key Conclusions

1. QNM bilinear product divergences are structural (due to CPT reflection), not merely geometric — hyperboloidal foliations clarify but don't eliminate them.
2. Multiple regularisation procedures yield consistent excitation coefficients.
3. The geometric framework connecting future/past foliations to CPT symmetry is conceptually valuable.
4. Scope limitation to scalar Schwarzschild leaves Kerr and tensor perturbations open.
